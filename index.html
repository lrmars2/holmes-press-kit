Feb – Aug 2022 · ADMINISTRATIVE · CRITICAL

Register Lists Home & Changes Locks During Bankruptcy Stay

Daniel Register of Northrop Realty lists Plaintiff’s home for sale in February 2022. A sign falsely stating the property was “bank/corporate owned and not for rent” is posted in the window.

Plaintiff files a formal complaint with the Maryland Real Estate Commission (MREC) against Register and Northrop Realty, alleging unauthorized leasing and management during an active bankruptcy stay. A $250,000 Guaranty Fund claim is filed simultaneously with 14 pages of evidence.

Late 2023 · ADMINISTRATIVE

Register’s 88-Page MREC Response — Critical Admission

Register files an 88-page response. In a pivotal sworn admission, he states that he and Northrop Realty were “assigned to manage the Property by Shellpoint Mortgage Servicing.” He also submits Plaintiff’s personal lease agreement from a different address and rental payment history — documents he never explains.
